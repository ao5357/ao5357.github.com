If you need a web developer slash librarian, look no further.

Brad got his start in the working world mowing neighbors' lawns, and quickly added caddying in the Summer (briefly) and doing oil changes and cleaning up a local Midas to his resume. He's done data entry for a catalog company, landscaping, and a bunch of typical college jobs like front desk attendant, dorm/apartment internet technician, and bookstore cashier, among others.

While studying Economics at Wayne State University in Detroit, he took an introductory Library Science course, and quickly applied to library school for graduate studies. While in grad school and for 5 years thereafter, Brad worked as a librarian with a technological bent, making websites and other code tools for public libraries: a 'clicker' for tracking reference interactions, web archives for digitized local history collections, improved ILS/catalog web interfaces, and solid (and responsive in 2010!) websites.

In early 2013, Brad saw an opportunity to help his friend Alex grow a small Drupal-based agency, Commercial Progression, into something wilder. CP was so successful, by 2016 it was acquired by Symmetri Marketing out of Chicago. Brad stayed on for 4 more years, becoming the Director of Technology for the 30+ person agency before eventually setting out on his own.

Today, Brad runs Solve it once. so1ve is Brad's vision for what a modern small Drupal agency can be: agile, independent, cost-effective, customer focused, with low overhead and a better work/life balance.

Ok, so the two sections above are great and all, but I think this section will be easier if I switch to first-person. What follows may not be the pithy marketing-speak like before. Sorry for the narrative whiplash.

I was born to Linda and Steve Czerniak in 1985. I grew up in Troy, Michigan, having attended Troy Union for preschool, Barnard Elementary, Baker Middle School, and Troy Athens High School in the class of 2003.

Early years

My first memory was visiting our former neighbors a few towns over. My older brother, Jeff, was really excited to go back there, though I was mostly apprehensive. The neighbor ended up being very nice, and I recall specifically standing next to a piano for a few minutes.

Not long after that, I vaguely recall the roughhousing incident in our family room when I fell off the couch with some force, and cut open my forehead on the ledge of the fireplace. Those were my first stitches, and the scar remains on my forehead, though nowadays it blends right in with my rapidly-deepening wrinkles.

My younger brother, Greg, needed eye surgery when he was 3 or 4 years old. He came out of it no worse for wear, and he received a Ghostbusters fire house playset from my parents that became the base of operations for all our action figures for years. If the Ghostbusters were real, they likely would have been perturbed by all the Ninja Turtles and Starfleet officers hanging out in their offices.
